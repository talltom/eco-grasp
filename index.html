#GRASP

An experiment to crowd-source urban data using Twitter

Mapping Fluvial Landscape Ecology using Social Media

Ciliwung River, Jakarta

Free Text as a Sensor Platform

The study presents a novel prototype for the semi-supervised crowd-sourced collection of coarse landscape-level biodiversity data. Data was collected via the social media platform Twitter and coupled urban data collection platform CogniCity.

In March 2014 trained volunteers boated down a 7.8 km section of the Ciliwung River in the city of Jakarta, Indonesia, and recorded land use data as per pre-specified landscape type categories. Reports were of unrestricted typology and included text as well as photographs, and geolocation metadata. By sending tweets to @petajkt with the hashtag #grasp, the CogniCity open source software registered reports for mapping and analysis.

The methodological simplicity of this Twitter-based experimental approach and accessibility of the software enables communities – particularly non-specialists – to actively contribute to local landscape mapping projects.
